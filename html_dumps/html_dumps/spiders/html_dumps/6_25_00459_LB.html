  • Some applications might not be decided solely by the Planning Officer and will be referred to Planning and Committee for recommended decision e.g. cases with significant objections, councillor involvement, contentious cases, cases with legal obligations attached

Decided

  • The Planning Department issues a Decision Notice informing the Applicant (or their agent) of the outcome of the application

You can make a comment supporting or objecting to this application. Your comment will be registered against the application and acknowledged electronically if you provide an email address. Please do not include personal information in your comments (e.g. name, address, telephone number, etc.).

When making comments on an application please note that whilst your name will not be displayed, your comments will be publicly available. Note that in the event of an appeal being lodged, we are obliged to forward your comments with your address to the Planning Inspectorate who will share this information with the Applicant/Appellant (Planning Inspectorate Privacy Statement).

Please be aware that once you have submitted your comment there will be a delay of up to three working days before the comment is published on the online Planning Register. The delay is necessary to allow the council to redact personal information as well as any derogatory or offensive content from the comment.

This web form will time out after 20 minutes and you will lose any comments that have not been submitted. Our advice is to draft your comments in another programme (notepad or MS Word) and then copy and paste your comments to this web form.

The Planning, Transport and Development Service is committed to protecting your privacy. Find out by reading our privacy notice.

Fri 14 Feb 2025
Validated
Fri 14 Feb 2025
Consultation
Recommendation and/or Committee
Decided

Received

  • Application is received by the department either electronically online or via post

Validated

  • The applicationhas been checked for completeness including things such as:
    • The application form has been completed correctly
    • Required supporting documents are present and correct
    • Site Location plan is present
    • Appropriate fee is paid
    • Applicant (or their agent) is contacted to acknowledge the application has been accepted

Consultation

  • The Local Authority will inform relevant parties of your application and invite views. This can include:
    • Putting up site notices
    • Advertising in local media
    • Writing to neighbours
    • Liasing with Statutory bodies such as Sewerage Undertakers

Recommendation and/or Committee

  • The Planning Officer checks the application against local policies and other considerations e.g. aesthetics, noise, etc.
  • The Planning Officer checks the views of local residents and statutory bodies who have been liaised with
  • The Planning Officer may undertake a site visit
  • Some applications might not be decided solely by the Planning Officer and will be referred to Planning and Committee for recommended decision e.g. cases with significant objections, councillor involvement, contentious cases, cases with legal obligations attached

Decided

  • The Planning Department issues a Decision Notice informing the Applicant (or their agent) of the outcome of the application
  • The decision is published e.g. in an online register such as Public Access
