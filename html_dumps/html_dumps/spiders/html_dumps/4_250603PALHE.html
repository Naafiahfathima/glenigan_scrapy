Received

  • Application is received by the department either electronically online or via post

Validated

  • The applicationhas been checked for completeness including things such as:
    • The application form has been completed correctly
    • Required supporting documents are present and correct
    • Site Location plan is present
    • Appropriate fee is paid
    • Applicant (or their agent) is contacted to acknowledge the application has been accepted

Consultation

  • The Local Authority will inform relevant parties of your application and invite views. This can include:
    • Putting up site notices
    • Advertising in local media
    • Writing to neighbours
    • Liasing with Statutory bodies such as Sewerage Undertakers

Recommendation and/or Committee

  • The Planning Officer checks the application against local policies and other considerations e.g. aesthetics, noise, etc.
  • The Planning Officer checks the views of local residents and statutory bodies who have been liaised with
  • The Planning Officer may undertake a site visit
  • Some applications might not be decided solely by the Planning Officer and will be referred to Planning and Committee for recommended decision e.g. cases with significant objections, councillor involvement, contentious cases, cases with legal obligations attached

Decided

  • The Planning Department issues a Decision Notice informing the Applicant (or their agent) of the outcome of the application
  • The decision is published e.g. in an online register such as Public Access

Make a Comment

Please complete the form below, and press Submit to pass your comments to the Planning Team. Any comments made will be published along with your address. Anonymous comments will not be considered. These comments will be available online and can be seen by other visitors to the Planning website. Any comments should be submitted in a rational and impersonal way and comments directed to the planning issues raised by the proposal. Please note that comments of a personal, libelous, defamatory, or otherwise offensive or abusive nature are not constructive or helpful to the process of determining a planning application and may result in an action for libel against you by third parties.  The Council cannot be held legally responsible for representations from anyone commenting on planning applications which may contain defamatory statements.

Received

  • Application is received by the department either electronically online or via post

Validated

  • The applicationhas been checked for completeness including things such as:
    • The application form has been completed correctly
    • Required supporting documents are present and correct
    • Site Location plan is present
    • Appropriate fee is paid
    • Applicant (or their agent) is contacted to acknowledge the application has been accepted

Consultation

  • The Local Authority will inform relevant parties of your application and invite views. This can include:
    • Putting up site notices
    • Advertising in local media
    • Writing to neighbours
    • Liasing with Statutory bodies such as Sewerage Undertakers

Recommendation and/or Committee

  • The Planning Officer checks the application against local policies and other considerations e.g. aesthetics, noise, etc.
  • The Planning Officer checks the views of local residents and statutory bodies who have been liaised with
  • The Planning Officer may undertake a site visit
  • Some applications might not be decided solely by the Planning Officer and will be referred to Planning and Committee for recommended decision e.g. cases with significant objections, councillor involvement, contentious cases, cases with legal obligations attached

Decided

  • The Planning Department issues a Decision Notice informing the Applicant (or their agent) of the outcome of the application
  • The decision is published e.g. in an online register such as Public Access
